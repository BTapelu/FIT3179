Northern Territory Car Fatalities by Speed Limit

figure 5

The graph tells it like it is: as speed limits go up in the NT, so do the car wrecks. No shocker, considering the wide-open spaces. Surprisingly low casualties in the 90-100km/h range, but here's the deal - outside built-up areas, the default is 110km/h, making those 90-100km/h roads rare. Now, take a look at >100km/h — boom, the numbers spike. It's the outback reality check. Maybe it's time the government tap the brakes on those speed limits for safer roads and fewer heartbreaks.

Northern Territory Car Fatalities by Time of Day

figure 6

The numbers don't lie—when the sun takes a break, danger hits the road in the NT. Nighttime accounts for a hefty 55.7% of car fatalities, with daytime trailing at 44.3%. Why the dark divide? Theories swirl — maybe stricter rules for nighttime drivers, like enforcing a passenger rule for young ones, could help. Shedding light on secluded roads with more poles might guide the way. And let's not forget the silent killer — DUI. A campaign for sober driving could be the beacon in the night for safer roads.
